Understanding Glaucoma

What is Glaucoma?

Glaucoma is a group of eye conditions that damage the optic nerve, vital for good vision. This damage is often caused by abnormally high pressure in your eye.

Types of Glaucoma

  • Open-Angle Glaucoma: The most common form, where the eye does not drain fluid as well as it should.
  • Angle-Closure Glaucoma: A less common form where the iris is close to the drainage angle in the eye. It can cause sudden buildup of pressure in the eye.

Recognizing Glaucoma

Early glaucoma has no symptoms. It's important to have regular eye exams that include measurements of your eye pressure. If glaucoma is recognized early, vision loss can be slowed or prevented.

Prevention Tips

While glaucoma can't be prevented, you can help detect it early by going for regular comprehensive eye exams. Protecting your eyes from injuries and controlling your blood pressure can also help reduce your risk.

Living with Glaucoma

Managing glaucoma involves taking your prescribed medicine, attending follow-up appointments with your eye doctor, and monitoring your vision with regular eye tests.

Support Resources

For more information and support, consider visiting the websites of the Glaucoma Research Foundation or the National Eye Institute. These resources offer educational materials, support groups, and tips for living with glaucoma.

Contact your doctor for more information about glaucoma or if you suspect you are experiencing symptoms.
